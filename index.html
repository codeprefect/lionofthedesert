Omar Al-Mukhtar (1793 - 1864)

teacher of the Qur'an, Ruler of Senussi Zawiyas

Below are the key highlights in the life of Omar Al-Mukhtar

  • 1858 - born on 20 August at Zawiyat Zanzur, Nahiyah Tobruk, Kaza Derna, Sanjak Benghazi, Eyalet-i Trâblus Gârb, Ottoman Empire to Al-Mukhtar ibn Muhammad and Aisha Bint Muharib.
  • 1895 - traveled with Al-Mahdi Senoussi to Kufra.
  • 1899 - fought among the Senussites anti-invasion forces.
  • 1902 - appointed as Sheikh of the troubled Zawiyat Laqsur in Northern Cyrenaica by the new Senussi leader Ahmed Sharif as-Senussi.
  • 1911 - started leading the Libyan Armed Opposition Forces.
  • 1927 - surprised Italian troops at Raheiba.
  • 1928 - reorganized the Senusite forces. General Teruzzi recognized Omar's qualities of "exceptional perseverance and strong will power.
  • 1929 - re-established a unity of action among Libyan forces, preparing himself for the ultimate confrontation with General Rodolfo Graziani,
  • 1931 - ambushed near Slonta and captured by Italian forces.
  • 1931 - 16 September (aged 73) at Suluq, Italian Libya and buried in Benghazi, Libya
  • 1981 - the movie "Lion of the Desert", starring Anthony Quinn, Oliver Reed, and Irene Papas was released depicting the struggles of Mukhtar against Rodolfo Graziani's forces.
  • 2009 - Libyan former leader Muammar Gaddafi wore a photograph of Mukhtar in Italian captivity on his chest while on a state visit to Rome, and brought along Mukhtar's elderly son during the visit.
